Introducing Declan Lighthouse

Declan Lighthouse is a documentary filmmaker and graduate student journalist at CUNY Newmark School of Journalism. Declan likes watching verite documentaries and reading long-form investigative journalism.

Here are some of his favorites:

  • Harlan County U.S.A (1976) directed by Barbara Kopple

    • Few pieces of media and/or literature have affected my politics and worldview as much as this film did when I first watched it in college. Being raised a San Francisco Liberal, this film turned me into a redneck Communist.

  • Say Nothing by Patrick Radden Keefe

    • Colonialism happened to Irish people too, and it was really, really bad. I only just read this one but I had to include it because it was so good. Long story short, sometimes revolutionaries make bad choices, but when something really, really bad happens, it's usually safe to assume the Imperialists are at fault.

  • The Prison in 12 Landscapes (2016) directed by Brett Story

    • This one is just so good. It demonstrates the kind of poetic subtly I aspire to achieve with my own filmmaking.
